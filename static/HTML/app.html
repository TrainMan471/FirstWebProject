What are the origins of the Rosary?

There are several stories of how the Rosary originated. There is evidence of it appearing even in Pre-Christian times, in which repeated prayers and beads were used to help someone meditate. There is even evidence from the Middle Ages that these strings of prayer beads were used to say Our Fathers and Hail Marys. The strings of beads used to say these prayers became known as "Paternosers", which means "Our Father."

Between the 12th and 15th centuries, the structure of the rosary evolved and now 50 "Hail Marys" were recited along with the Our Fathers. The 50 Hail Marys were recited with either reflections of the lives of Jesus and Mary, or with verses of the psalms. This form of prayer became known as the rosarium(which means "rose garden"). This was a common term during this time that was used to designate collections of similar things, for example a collection of stories about the same subject.When the 16th century rolled around, a structure of the five decade rosary based on three sets of mysteries(Joyful, Glorious,and Sorrowful) flourished. However, the most accepted origin of the rosary is associated with St.Dominic.

Tradition holds that St.Dominic devised the rosary as we know it. Deeply moved by a vision of our Blessed Mother, he preached about the use of the rosary in his missionary work, and in his sermons.However, some scholars believe that this origin is a little sketchy - early accounts of St.Dominic's life do not mention the Rosary, Dominican records don't associate him with it, and many contemporary portraits don't include as a means to identify the saint. The rosary seems to have been developed over a long and gradual process, being developed over many centuries. However, many popes have associated St.Dominic with the rosary, stating "If nothing else, his role is at least a 'pious belief'"

Mysteries of the Most Holy Rosary

The are four sets of mysteries for the most Holy Rosary:

  • The Sorrowful Mysteries
  • The Joyful Mysteries
  • The Glorious Mysteries
  • The Luminous Mysteries

Each with their own set of mysteries to think about and contemplate on:

The Sorrowful Mysteries

  • First Sorrowful Mystery: The Agony in the Garden
    • Meditation:
    In His anguish, He prayed with all the greater intensity, and His sweat became like drops of blood falling to the ground.The He rose from prayer and came to His disciples, only to find them asleep, exhausted with grief(Lk 22:44-45)
  • The Second Sorrowful Mystery:The Scourging at the Pillar
    • Meditation:
    Pilate's next move was to take Jesus and have Him scourged(Jn 19:1)
  • The Third Sorrowful Mystery:The Crowning With Thorns
    • Meditation:
    They stripped off His clothes and wrapped Him in a scarlet military cloak. Weaving a crown out of thorns they fixed it on His head, and stuck a reed in His right hand(Mt 27: 28 - 29)
  • The Fourth Sorrowful Mystery:The Carrying Of The Cross
    • Meditation:
    ...Carrying the cross by Himself, He went out to what is called the Place of the Skull. (In Hebrew, this was known as Golgotha)(Jn 19:17)
  • The Fifth Sorrowful Mystery:The Crucifixion>
    • Meditation:
    Jesus uttered a loud cry and said, "Father, into Your hands I commend My Spirit." After He said this, He expired.(Lk 23:46)

    The Glorious Mysteries

  • The First Glorious Mystery:The Resurrection
    • Meditation:
    You need not be amazed! You are looking for Jesus of Nazareth, the one who was crucified.He has been raised up; He is not here.See the place where they laid Him. (Mk 16:6)
  • The Second Glorious Mystery: The Ascension
    • Meditation:
    Then, after speaking to them, the Lord Jesus was taken up into heaven and rook His seat at God's right hand.(Mk 16:19)
  • The Third Glorious Mystery:The Descent of The Holy Spirit
    • Meditation:
    All were filled with the Holy Spirit.They began to express themselves in foreign tongues and make bold proclamation as the Spirit prompted them.(Acts 2:4)
  • The Fourth Glorious Mystery:The Assumption of Our Lady
    • Meditation
    You are the glory of Jerusalem...you are the splendid boast of our people.. God is pleased with what you have wrought.May you be blessed by the Lord almighty forever and ever.(Jth 15: 9 - 10)
  • The Fifth Glorious Mystery:The Cornoation of Our Lady
    • Meditation:
    A great sign appeared in the sky, a women clothed with the sun, with the moon under her feet, and on her head a crown of twelve stars.(Rev 12:1)

  • The First Joyful Mystery: The Annunciation
    • Meditation:

  • The Second Joyful Mystery:The Visitation
    • Meditation:
    Elizabeth was filled with the Holy Spirit and cried out in a loud voice: "Blest are you among women and blest is the fruit of your womb.(Lk:1:41 - 42)
  • The Third Joyful Mystery:The Birth of Jesus
    • Meditation:

  • The Fourth Joyful Mystery:The Presentation

  • The Fifth Joyful Mystery:The Finding of The Child Jesus in the Temple
    • On the third day they came upon Him in the temple sitting in the midst of the teachers, listening to them and asking them questions.(Lk 2:46)

    The Luminous Mysteries

  • The First Luminous Mystery:The Baptism of Jesus in the Jordan
    • Meditation:
    And when Jesus was baptized, the heavens were opened and He saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove, and alighting on Him, and lo, a voice from heaven saying "This is my beloved son, with whom I am well pleased." (Mt 3: 16 - 17)
  • The Second Luminous Mystery: The Wedding Feast at Cana
    • Meditation:
    His mother said to the servants," Do whatever He tells you". Jesus said to them, "Fill the jars with water." And they filled them to the brim.(Jn 2: 5 - 7)
  • The Third Luminous Mystery: The Proclamation of the Kingdom
    • Meditation:
    "And preach as you go, saying, 'The Kingdom of Heaven is at hand.'Heal the sick, raise the dead,cleanse the lepers, cast out demons.You recieved without pay, give without pay."(Mt 10:7 - 8)
  • The Fourth Luminous Mystery: The Transfiguration
    • Meditation:
    And as he was praying, the appearance of his countenance was altered and His raminent became dazzling white. And a voice came out of the cloud saying, "This is my beloved Son,my chosen, listen to Him!"(Lk 9:29,35)
  • The Fifth Luminous Mystery: The Institution of the Eucharist
    • Meditation:
    And He took bread, and when he had given thanks He broke it and gave it to them saying, "This is my body which is given for you."...And likewise the cup after supper, saying, "This cup which is poured out for you is the new covenant in My blood"(Lk 22:19 - 20)
